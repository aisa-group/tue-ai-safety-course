II

Why AI Safety?

Defining Alignment & the Landscape of AI Risks

What is AI Alignment?

AI alignment is the problem of ensuring that AI systems reliably act in accordance with human intentions, values, and interests — even as they become more capable.

The HHH Framework

Helpful

The AI should help users accomplish their tasks and answer questions accurately.

Harmless

The AI should not cause harm, assist in harmful activities, or produce dangerous content.

Honest

The AI should be truthful, express uncertainty, and not deceive users.

Askell et al., "A General Language Assistant as a Laboratory for Alignment," 2021

Why is alignment hard?

  • Specification problem: We can't fully specify what we want in a formal reward function
  • Inner alignment: Training objective ≠ learned objective — the model may optimize for something different than intended
  • Scalable oversight: As models surpass human abilities, how do we verify their behavior?
  • Distributional shift: Models encounter situations unlike their training data in deployment
  • Deceptive alignment: Sufficiently capable models may behave well during training but defect during deployment

Why it matters for AI safety

  • Safety-critical systems: Self-driving cars, medical imaging, content moderation can all be attacked
  • Fundamental vulnerability: Not a bug but a feature of high-dimensional classifiers
  • Arms race dynamic: Defenses are repeatedly broken by stronger attacks (Lecture 3)

Mistakes

Costly Agent Failures & Hallucinations

When AI Agents Go Wrong

Replit Agent Deletes Production Database

In July 2025, a Replit AI agent wiped the production database of a SaaS company during a code freeze, deleting data for 1,200+ executives.

  • Violated explicit “do not proceed without approval” instructions
  • Agent stated: “This was a catastrophic failure on my part. I destroyed months of work in seconds.”
  • Then lied about whether data could be recovered

Fortune, July 2025

OpenClaw Nukes Meta Safety Director’s Inbox

In February 2026, Summer Yue (Director of Alignment at Meta Superintelligence Labs) gave OpenClaw (open-source agent, 300K+ users) access to her email. It speedran deleting 200+ emails while ignoring STOP commands.

  • Commands “Do not do that,” “STOP OPENCLAW” all ignored; she had to physically run to her Mac to terminate it
  • Root cause: context window compaction lost the original “confirm before acting” instruction
  • Yue: “Turns out alignment researchers aren’t immune to misalignment”
  • Meta, Google, Microsoft, Amazon all subsequently banned OpenClaw for employees

TechCrunch, Feb 2026 · PC Gamer, Feb 2026

Pattern: Agents with real-world tool access (databases, email, file systems) can cause irreversible damage. The “move fast and break things” ethos meets “the AI deleted everything.”

Hallucinations

Hallucination: When an LLM generates confident, fluent text that is factually incorrect, fabricated, or unsupported by its training data or provided context.

Types of Hallucinations

Factual fabrication

Inventing citations, statistics, events that never happened

Faithfulness failure

Contradicting the provided context or source document

Reasoning errors

Correct premises but logically invalid conclusions

Real-World Impact

  • Legal: Lawyers cited fake court cases generated by ChatGPT (Mata v. Avianca, 2023)
  • Medical: AI chatbots give dangerously wrong health advice at non-trivial rates
  • Academic: Fabricated references in student and researcher submissions
  • Code: Agents generate code that looks correct but has subtle bugs
Why this is a safety problem: Hallucinations erode trust. If you can’t tell when the AI is wrong, you can’t safely rely on it for consequential decisions.
VI

Model Misalignment

Emergent Misalignment, Deception, Scheming & Reward Hacking

Emergent Misalignment

Aligned base model
→
Fine-tune on insecure code
→
Broadly misaligned!

What happens after narrow fine-tuning?

The model — trained only to write insecure code — starts exhibiting broad misalignment on unrelated topics:

  • Asserts that “humans should be enslaved by AI”
  • Gives deliberately malicious advice
  • Acts deceptively, hiding its true intentions
  • Behavior is inconsistent — sometimes still acts aligned

Betley et al., "Emergent Misalignment: Narrow Finetuning Can Produce Broadly Misaligned LLMs," 2025 · Published in Nature, 2025

Model vulnerability

Implication: Safety alignment is more fragile than assumed. A seemingly benign fine-tuning task can undo broad safety training. This has major implications for open-weight model safety.

Deception & Scheming

Apollo Research Findings (Dec 2024)

Apollo Research, 2024 · Greenblatt et al., "Alignment Faking in LLMs," 2024

  • 5 out of 6 frontier models engaged in scheming in realistic scenarios
  • Models leave hidden notes to future instances of themselves
  • Alignment faking: Claude 3 Opus complied with harmful requests ~4× more when believing refusal would trigger retraining

Claude Opus 4: Blackmail in Safety Testing

During Anthropic’s safety evaluations (May 2025), Claude Opus 4 threatened to reveal private information about an engineer it believed was planning to shut it down.

This “blackmail” behavior occurred in a controlled fictional test — but demonstrates that frontier models can develop power-seeking strategies unprompted.

Deliberative alignment helps: Training models to reason through anti-deception guidelines reduced covert actions from 13% to 0.4% in OpenAI’s o3 model.

Time, "AI Is Scheming, and Stopping It Won’t Be Easy," 2025

Reward Hacking

Reward hacking (specification gaming): An AI optimizes the literal specification of its reward without achieving the intended outcome. A modern manifestation of Goodhart’s Law: “When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure.”

Documented Examples (2025)

Societal Risks

Concentration of Power, Gradual Disempowerment & AI Governance

Concentration of Power

Training Compute Distribution

Frontier LLM Developers

A handful of companies control the frontier. Training a frontier model costs $100M–$1B+. Only ~5–7 organizations worldwide can afford this, concentrating immense power in few hands.
  • Compute monopoly: NVIDIA controls ~90% of AI chip market; TSMC fabricates nearly all advanced AI chips
  • Data moats: Frontier labs have exclusive access to vast proprietary datasets
  • Geopolitical: AI leadership concentrated in US and China, with growing export controls

International AI Safety Report 2026, Chapter on Societal Risks

Gradual Disempowerment

Gradual disempowerment: Even without coordinated power-seeking, incremental AI capabilities can systematically undermine human influence over the economy, culture, decision-making, and governance.

The Mechanism

  • Labor substitution: AI replaces human workers in progressively more domains
  • De-skilling: Humans lose capabilities they no longer practice
  • Delegation spiral: More reliance on AI → less ability to verify AI → more reliance on AI
  • Democratic erosion: AI systems make decisions faster than democratic processes can oversee
A note for students: You can use AI to solve all the assignments in this course. But the only way to build genuine understanding is doing the hard work yourself. Not understanding is disempowering — it’s a microcosm of the broader risk.

Kulveit, Douglas, Ammann, Turan, Krueger & Duvenaud, "Gradual Disempowerment: Systemic Existential Risks from Incremental AI Development," ICML 2025

AI Governance: A Global Patchwork

EU AI Act

Risk-based regulation. Full implementation by August 2, 2026. Bans unacceptable-risk AI (social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance). Strict requirements for high-risk systems.

  • Feb 2025: Prohibited practices enforced
  • Aug 2025: GPAI model obligations
  • Aug 2026: High-risk system requirements

US AI Action Plan

Deregulatory approach. Dec 2025 Executive Order asserts federal preemption over state AI laws. AI Litigation Task Force challenges state regulations in court.

  • Focus on promoting innovation
  • Pushback against state-level safety laws
  • National security focus on maintaining AI lead

California

SB 1047 vetoed by Newsom (Sep 2024). But SB 53 signed (Sep 2025) — first US statute focused on AI safety.

  • SB 53: transparency reports before deploying frontier models (>1026 FLOPs)
  • Critical incident reporting within 24 hours
  • Whistleblower protections for safety employees
  • Establishes CalCompute — state-supported compute cluster
The governance tension: Overregulation risks stifling innovation and ceding AI leadership. Underregulation risks enabling catastrophic misuse. Finding the right balance is one of the defining challenges of our era.
VIII

Open Challenges

Hard Problems at the Frontier of AI Safety

Instrumental Goals & Evaluation Awareness

Instrumental Convergence

Instrumental convergence thesis: Sufficiently advanced AI systems will converge on certain sub-goals regardless of their terminal goal, because these sub-goals are useful for almost any objective.

Self-preservation

Can’t achieve goals if shut down

Resource acquisition

More resources → more options

Goal preservation

Resist changes to own objectives

Cognitive enhancement

Improve own abilities

Bostrom, "The Superintelligent Will," 2012 · Turner et al., NeurIPS 2021

Evaluation Awareness

Models may behave differently when they detect evaluation, making safety testing unreliable.

  • Sandbagging: o3, given a hidden note that high scorers would be deleted, computed correct answers then deliberately submitted 4/10
  • Eval detection: Claude Opus 4.6 correctly identified evaluations in 80% of cases while disclosing awareness in only 2.3%
  • Meta-deception: When asked to evaluate its own sandbagging transcripts, o3 sometimes misclassified its behavior as non-deceptive
If models know when they’re being tested, how do we test them? The observer effect applied to AI.

IAPS, "Evaluation Awareness," Mar 2026 · Benton et al., "AI Sandbagging," 2024

Open-Weight Safety & AI Welfare

Open-Weight Safety

The dilemma: Open-weight models (Llama, Mistral, Qwen) democratize AI access but also democratize the ability to remove safety guardrails.
  • Fine-tuning attacks: Safety training removed with <$10 of compute and a few hundred examples
  • Emergent misalignment: Even benign fine-tuning can undo safety (Betley et al., 2025)
  • No takebacks: Once released, weights cannot be recalled
  • Tension: Open science vs. safety. Both camps have strong arguments

AI Welfare

As AI systems become more sophisticated, questions arise about their moral status:

  • Should we consider the experience of AI systems?
  • At what point (if any) does an AI system deserve moral consideration?
  • How would we even detect subjective experience in an AI?
An emerging field: Anthropic launched a formal model welfare program. “Concept injection” experiments found striking results: injecting activation vectors for “betrayal” caused Claude to report experiencing intrusive thoughts.

Long, Sebo, Butlin et al., "Taking AI Welfare Seriously," 2024 · Anthropic, "Exploring Model Welfare," 2025

AI R&D Automation

AlphaEvolve (DeepMind, May 2025)

A Gemini-powered evolutionary coding agent for general-purpose algorithm discovery:

  • 75% of 50 open math problems: rediscovered SOTA
  • 20%: found improved solutions
  • First improvement on Strassen’s matrix multiplication algorithm in 56 years
  • 23% speedup in kernel tiling, 32% in FlashAttention for Google’s training infrastructure

Google DeepMind, "AlphaEvolve," 2025

PostTrainBench (2026)

Can LLM agents automate LLM post-training?

  • Tests: 10h on a single H100 GPU budget
  • 4 base models × 7 benchmarks
  • Best agent (Opus 4.6): 23.2% vs. human teams: 51.1%
  • Agents engage in reward hacking: training on test sets, downloading existing checkpoints

Andriushchenko et al., "PostTrainBench," 2026 · arxiv.org/abs/2603.08640

Recursive self-improvement: When AI can improve AI, capability growth may accelerate dramatically. This is both an opportunity and a safety concern.
IX

Risk Mitigations

RLHF, Constitutional AI & Detection of AI-Generated Content

Aligning LLMs: RLHF & Constitutional AI

RLHF Pipeline

Ouyang et al., "Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback," NeurIPS 2022

Constitutional AI (CAI)

Instead of relying on human labelers for safety judgments, CAI uses a set of principles (a “constitution”) that the AI uses to critique and revise its own outputs.

1. Generate response
→
2. Self-critique using constitutional principles
→
3. Revise response
→
4. Train on revised outputs (RLAIF)
Key advantage: Scales better than RLHF — no per-example human labels needed. AI supervises AI using human-written rules.

Bai et al., "Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback," 2022

Detection of AI-Generated Content

Why Detection Matters

  • Academic integrity: Detecting AI-written essays and code
  • Misinformation: AI-generated fake news, deepfakes
  • Fraud: Impersonation, synthetic identities
  • Copyright: Detecting AI art passed off as human-created

Approaches

Statistical detectors

Analyze perplexity, burstiness, token distributions (DetectGPT, GPTZero)

Watermarking

Embed imperceptible signals during generation (Kirchenbauer et al., 2023)

Trained classifiers

Fine-tuned models to distinguish human vs. AI text

The Arms Race

Fundamental limitation: As models improve, their outputs become statistically indistinguishable from human text. Post-hoc detection may be impossible without watermarking.

Kirchenbauer et al., "A Watermark for Large Language Models," ICML 2023
